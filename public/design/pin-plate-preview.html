Pinned photo — the view stays vivid, only the text gets a plate

Instead of darkening the whole card, the photo is shown at full vividness and the text sits on a small rounded darkened box (a “scrim panel”). Use the toolbar to try the plate hugging the text vs a full-width inset bar, solid vs frosted glass, and how dark the plate is — the readout keeps it honest (light ink stays ≥AA over any photo).

Notes

  • The photo is no longer filtered or scrimmed — it renders at full vividness.
  • Only .pin-body (the text block) gets a darkened rounded background; everything else is the untouched photo.
  • Readability is still guaranteed: the plate tint alone (ignoring the photo) clears AA — the readout shows the worst case, a pure-white photo behind the text.
  • Frosted glass is optional polish; the tint (not the blur) carries the contrast, and it’s dropped automatically under “reduced transparency”.
