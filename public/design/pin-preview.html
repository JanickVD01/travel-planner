Why the text is always readable

  • Light ink sits on a warm-black scrim whose alpha never drops below --pin-floor (default 0.82) anywhere text can be. Worst case = a pure-white photo — see the live readout above (≈ 11:1 title, ≈ 9:1 dates at the default).
  • The scrim is anchored to the text, not the card — so it covers exactly the text at any card height or title length, and the photo breathes above it.
  • A single lazy <img> (not background-image) means off-screen photos don't download — fast on mobile.
  • Status chips and the spine + markers are untouched (chips carry their own opaque AA-safe background). One accent only; the photo is content, not decoration.
  • If a pinned photo is deleted or fails to load, the card reverts to the normal surface — no unreadable state ever.
